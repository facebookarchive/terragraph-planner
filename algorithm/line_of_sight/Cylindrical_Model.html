Cylindrical Model

The cylindrical model is a simplified model from the ellipsoidal model. Given the fact that the maximum radius of Fresnel zone of a 60 GHz radio is small, which is usually less than 1 meter, the cylindrical model is able to give a credible result when using most geographical data whose resolution is about 1 meter too. In the cylindrical model, the radius is uniform everywhere, called “Fresnel radius”, and is inputed by users.

Problem Modeling​

The main problem of this model is to compute the distance in a 3D space. We compute the distance from DSM grid (or pixel) to the LOS center line (the line from one end site to the other end site, without width) to determine is this grid blocks the Fresnel zone. We use the center of the DSM grid as representative to compute the distance. Thus, this problem becomes computing distance from a 3-D line segment to another 3-D line segment.

Math Equations​

Since computing the distance between two 3-D lines is difficult, let’s simplify the computation.

Say the two end sites have coordinates (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​), and one DSM grid (x0​,y0​)(x_0​,y_0​)(x0​​,y0​​) has the base at i0​i_0​i0​​ and the top at h (then the building height is ∣h−i0​∣∣h−i_0​∣∣h−i0​​∣).

Then the formula of the LOS center line is:

The line segment for the shortest distance between both lines (i.e., the line segment between (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2,y2​,z2​)(x_2,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​,y2​​,z2​​) and the line segment between (x0​,y0​,i0​)(x_0​,y_0​,i_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,i0​​) and (x0​,y0​,h)(x_0​,y_0​,h)(x0​​,y0​​,h) must be orthogonal. Suppose the intersection on the DSM grid and the line segment is (x0​,y0​,z0​)(x_0​,y_0​,z_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,z0​​), and the intersection on the LOS center line and the line segment is (x1​+t0​×p,y1​+t0​×q,z1​+t0​×r)(x_1​+t_0​ \times p,y_1​+t_0​ \times q,z_1​+t_0​ \times r)(x1​​+t0​​×p,y1​​+t0​​×q,z1​​+t0​​×r).

Then the shortest distance d2=(x0​−x1​−t0×p)2+(y0​−y1​−t0​×q)2+(z0​−z1​−t0​×r)2d^2=(x_0​−x_1​−t_0 \times p)^2+(y_0​−y_1​−t_0​\times q)^2+(z_0​−z_1​−t_0​ \times r)^2d2=(x0​​−x1​​−t0​×p)2+(y0​​−y1​​−t0​​×q)2+(z0​​−z1​​−t0​​×r)2.

where (x0​−x1​−t0​×p)×p+(y0​−y1​−t0​×q)×q+(z0​−z1​−t0​×r)×r=0(x_0​−x_1​−t_0​\times p) \times p+(y_0​−y_1​−t_0​\times q) \times q+(z_0​−z_1​−t_0​\times r)\times r=0(x0​​−x1​​−t0​​×p)×p+(y0​​−y1​​−t0​​×q)×q+(z0​​−z1​​−t0​​×r)×r=0, because of the orthogonality.

Then to simplify them, we get d2=(x0​−x1​−t0×p)2+(y0​−y1​−t0​×q)2d^2=(x_0​−x_1​−t_0\times p)^2+(y_0​−y_1​−t_0​\times q)^2d2=(x0​​−x1​​−t0​×p)2+(y0​​−y1​​−t0​​×q)2, where (x0​−x1​−t0​×p)×p+(y0​−y1​−t0​×q)×q=0(x_0​−x_1​−t_0​\times p)\times p+(y_0​−y_1​−t_0​ \times q)\times q=0(x0​​−x1​​−t0​​×p)×p+(y0​​−y1​​−t0​​×q)×q=0. It becomes a problem of computing the distance from a 2-D point (x0,y0)(x_0, y_0)(x0​,y0​) to a 2-D line from (x1​,y1​)(x_1​,y_1​)(x1​​,y1​​) to (x2​,y2​)(x_2​,y_2​)(x2​​,y2​​). The shapely lib can compute it quickly.

But what if z0​>hz_0​>hz0​​>h, which means the intersection point is actually higher than the DSM grid top? In this case, we need to compute the distance from the 3-D point (x0​,y0​,h)(x_0​,y_0​,h)(x0​​,y0​​,h) to the 3-D line between (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​). Then how to determine it? We can compute the formula of plane that goes through points (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​), (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​) and (x0​,y0​,z0​)(x_0​,y_0​,z_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,z0​​), and use the formula to get z0z_0z0​​, and then compare it with hhh. We already know that line between (x0​,y0​,z0​)(x_0​,y_0​,z_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,z0​​) and (x1​+t0​×p,y1​+t0​×q,z1​+t0​×r)(x_1​+t_0​\times p,y_1​+t_0​\times q,z_1​+t_0​\times r)(x1​​+t0​​×p,y1​​+t0​​×q,z1​​+t0​​×r) is orthogonal with the line between (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​), then we find another line that is parallel to the line between (x0​,y0​,z0​)(x_0​,y_0​,z_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,z0​​) and (x1​+t0​×p,y1​+t0​×q,z1​+t0​×r)(x_1​+t_0​\times p,y_1​+t_0​\times q,z_1​+t_0​\times r)(x1​​+t0​​×p,y1​​+t0​​×q,z1​​+t0​​×r) but intersects with (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​) to determine the plane that also contains (x0​,y0​,z0​)(x_0​,y_0​,z_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,z0​​). In that way, we can avoid computing the plane formula every time for each DSM pixel.

Finally, we get the process to compute the distance, which is implemented at los/cylindrical_los_validator.py.

Steps to Decide LOS​

For each pair of site (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​):

  1. (Step 1) In 2-D space, we find every DSM pixel (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) whose distance to the line between (x1​,y1​)(x_1​,y_1​)(x1​​,y1​​) and (x2​,y2​)(x_2​,y_2​)(x2​​,y2​​) is smaller than the Fresnel radius. The distance from each of those DSM pixels to the 3-D LOS center should be smaller than the Fresnel radius if we do not consider the height. We call it possible_obstructions in the codebase.
  2. (Step 2) Use (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​), (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​) and the third point (x3​,y3​,z3​)(x_3​,y_3​,z_3​)(x3​​,y3​​,z3​​), where the line between (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x3​,y3​,z3​)(x_3​,y_3​,z_3​)(x3​​,y3​​,z3​​) should be parallel to the line between (x0​,y0​,z0​)(x_0​,y_0​,z_0​)(x0​​,y0​​,z0​​) and (x1​+t0​×p,y1​+t0​×q,z1​+t0​×r)(x_1​+t_0​ \times p,y_1​+t_0​ \times q,z_1​+t_0​ \times r)(x1​​+t0​​×p,y1​​+t0​​×q,z1​​+t0​​×r) to compute the formula of the plane that contains the intersection point between DSM pixel and the orthogonal line. We call that plane as max_top_view_plane in codebase.
  3. (Step 3) Use the plane formula to check if z0​≤hz_0​ \leq hz0​​≤h.
    1. If z0​≤hz0​ \leq hz0​≤h, use shapely lib to compute the distance from point (x0​,y0​)(x_0​,y_0​)(x0​​,y0​​) to the line between (x1​,y1​)(x_1​,y_1​)(x1​​,y1​​) and (x2​,y2​)(x_2​,y_2​)(x2​​,y2​​), which is equal to the distance from DSM grid to the LOS center line.
    2. If z0​>hz0​>hz0​>h, compute the distance from (x0​,y0​,h)(x_0​,y_0​,h)(x0​​,y0​​,h) to the line between (x1​,y1​,z1​)(x_1​,y_1​,z_1​)(x1​​,y1​​,z1​​) and (x2​,y2​,z2​)(x_2​,y_2​,z_2​)(x2​​,y2​​,z2​​) as the final distance. Be aware of whether the intersection point is line within the LOS center line here, since LOS center line is not an infinite line.
  4. Finally, we use the shortest distance to compute the confidence level. If the confidence level is greater than the threshold, we propose that LOS link, otherwise reject it.
